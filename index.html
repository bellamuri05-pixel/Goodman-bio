Timothy Goodman is a New York-based designer and illustrator whose work centers vulnerability, mental health, and emotional honesty. Growing up in Cleveland with a single mother, he brings those early experiences into a practice focused on openness, connection, and community. Inspired by artists like Red Grooms and shaped by time in major design roles before going independent, Goodman uses hand-drawn typography, murals, public art, and storytelling to explore fears, heartbreak, masculinity, and the scars that shape it. He sees personal storytelling as a form of activism, using projects like “40 Days of Dating,” “12 Kinds of Kindness,” and more than 250 public murals to spark empathy and dialogue. By acknowledging his privilege and choosing to amplify others, he shows how design can be emotionally meaningful and socially engaged. His work encourages others to embrace vulnerability as a creative strength and to see design as a space for genuine emotional connection.
